What is Snorkelology?

Hello and welcome to Snorkelology! We are Emma and Gordon, and we are big fans of spending time outdoors particularly in, on and around the water. One of our favourite things to do is exploring the seas around the British coastline armed with a mask and snorkel, or even just a pair of goggles...

Snorkelology is for all those interested in exploring the marine environment around Britain, with or without a snorkel.

We had our first exposure to the wonders of British sea life through SCUBA diving. Then came the challenge of babies and our water-based forays became more family focussed, with sneaky swims on family beach days. It was these quick dips with nothing but a pair of goggles which reminded us that you don't need lots of kit to explore the beautiful underwater world.

We launched Snorkelology as a way to connect with others who want to find out more about British Snorkelling...

Increasingly we began to pack our mask and snorkel on our family trips, the kids joined in and we were reminded that snorkelling is a fun and accessible activity that the whole family can enjoy.

Let's bust some myths about British snorkelling ...

There is a lot of nonsense spoken about snorkelling in Britain, and in this section we try to dispel some of those myths!

Isn't it too cold to snorkel in Britain?

In the summer the water temperature can reach 20°C or higher, and this is the sort of temperature that some people can tolerate without a wetsuit. However if you want to be a year-round snorkeller, you will certainly need a wetsuit to protect you from the elements.

Is there anything to see snorkelling in Britain?

We have been diving, snorkelling and swimming around Britain for 30 years, and the emphatic response is YES! British waters are teeming with marine life. We have large mammals like seals and dolphins, large fish like sunfish and wrasse, along with a wealth of smaller critters (our favourite is the Tompot Blenny!) You do have to look a bit harder than on a tropical reef, but that game of hide-and-seek is part of the enjoyment.

Is snorkelling in Britain safe?

Snorkelling, like any water-based activity, comes with risks but there are many common-sense things we can do to enjoy the marine environment safely. Number one on our list will always be: never snorkel alone. It's so important that you have someone to look out for you, even if they stay on the shore. Be aware of your knowledge and experience level and seek advice from locals or experts as necessary.

Where are the best snorkelling sites in Britain?

Britain's coastline is rich and diverse, and you can find marine life almost anywhere. The locations most abundant with life tend to be those with rocky seabed with healthy currents: enough to bring nutrients but not too strong that fauna cannot hold on. Just make sure you check out the tides and currents before you pop in.

Is it 'snorkelling' or 'snorkeling'?

The Oxford English Dictionary gives 'snorkelling' as the correct spelling in British English.

Snorkelology is a bit of a tongue-twister, how did you find the name?

Yes, it is a bit of a tongue-twister! But we wanted a made-up name so it would be easy to find a domain name. We recommend thinking of it as a compound of 'snorkel' and the ending '-ology'.
